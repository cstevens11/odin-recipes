Vegan Nacho Cheese

Once you eat this, you'll think less of all other vegan cheeses.

As long as you have a blender, this is going to be one of your new favorite things to make. Replace real cheese in a nacho platter with this and all your vegan friends will fall madly in love with you while your non-vegan friends contemplate how non-cheese ingredients can work so well as a replacement.

Large group of people coming over? Just double the amount of the ingredients and make an even bigger batch!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup water from boiling carrots (save more in case consistency is too thick)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt + more if needed
  • 1/2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • pinch of cayenne

Steps

  1. Boil the carrots and the potatoes in separate pots until they are soft enough to easily poke through with a fork. Be sure to save all the water from the carrot pot (the potato water will not be needed).
  2. Add the potatoes, carrots, 2 tbsp olive oil, and 1/4 cup carrot water to the blender. Blend together until very smooth.
  3. Add remaining ingredients and blend until cheese is very smooth.
  4. Dip a chip or spoon into blender and pull up to check cheese consistency. If it does not pour like real nacho cheese add more of the reserved carrot water until desired consistency is reached.
  5. Taste cheese and adjust to perfection with salt, paprika, cayenne, etc. (I usually end up tossing in extra garlic/onion powder, cayenne and paprika until the perfect level of spicyness is achieved.
  6. Pour that blender of nacho gold onto whatever your heart desires and enjoy!
