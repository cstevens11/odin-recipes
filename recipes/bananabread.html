Banana Bread

Have bananas that are a bit too old to eat? Harvest their true worth.

Spend 15 minutes on prep right now, go vibe for an hour, and then follow your nose to the fruits (or fruit bread) of your labor. This easy recipe is perfect for a late night, a date night, or literally any other time of the day.

This banana bread is easy and simple; it should take about 15 minutes to mix everything together and another 50/60 minutes to bake. The extra time to let the bread cool is entirely up to you and your own burn tolerance.

Ingredients

  • 2 to 3 ripe bananas
  • 1/3 cup butter (salted or unsalted)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all purpose flour

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C), and butter a loaf pan.
  2. Mash ripe bananas in a mixing bowl with a fork until smooth.
  3. Stir melted butter into the mashed bananas.
  4. Mix in the baking soda and salt. Stir in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Mix in the flour until overall texture is consistent throughout.
  5. Pour the batter into buttered baking pan. Bake for 50/60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Remove from oven and let cool in pan for a few minutes before removing from pan. Allow more time for loaf to cool until desired temperature, then slice and enjoy.
